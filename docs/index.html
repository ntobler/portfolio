Probabilistic Modelling of Debris Risk for Space Flight

Project at Rocket Lab Ltd. 2024 - 2025

Rocket Lab is an aerospace manufacturer and small satellite launch service provider. To obtain a launch license from the Federal Aviation Administration (FAA), the Flight Analysis team at Rocket Lab develops in-house tools to demonstrate that the risk of potential debris from a launch vehicle is below the required threshold. The developed tool calculates high-resolution debris maps from Monte Carlo simulations of the launch vehicle trajectory to comply with the updated FAA requirements of 14 CFR Part 450. The tool programmed in Python with performance critical parts in C++ is integrated into the existing pipeline-based software architecture.

Camera-based Star Tracker Alignment Tool for Astro Photography

Personal Project 2024 - 2025 Project on GitHub

A Raspberry Pi based image processing project facilitates the alignment of a star tracker for astro photography.

Python Rust Raspberry Pi Linux NumPy OpenCV
UI showing the camera frame overlaid on the celestial sphere with the detected stars and the estimated position of the north pole relative to the alignment axis. Created by the author.

Astro photography requires long exposure times to capture faint objects in the night sky. To compensate for the star movement during the exposure, the camera can be rotated about the Earth's axis using a star tracker. On the northern hemisphere, the North Star (Polaris) can be used to precisely align the star tracker using an optical viewfinder. However, on the southern hemisphere, a more sophisticated approach is required, as the South Celestial Pole lacks a bright reference star.

A solution using a low-noise IMX462 camera module together with a Raspberry Pi Zero 2 W has been engineered to estimate the attitude of the star tracker's alignment axis from a camera image. A web application accessible through the Raspberry Pi's WiFi functionality has been developed to visualize the alignment in real-time. After pre-processing the raw image, the star positions and the resulting attitude is calculated using an algorithm proposed by Pedrotty et al., 2020 implemented in Rust and Python. The tool requires only coarse initial camera calibration data, as high-confidence measurements can be used to refine the calibration parameters. Furthermore, mechanical misalignment of the camera and the star tracker can be compensated for by using a simple calibration procedure.

With the hardware setup used, the algorithm is able to detect the attitude from star positions in a single image with a one-sigma precision of less than 0.01째. The absolute accuracy has not been quantified, however, field tests have shown that the alignment is accurate enough for multiple minute exposures without visible residual star movement.

3D Measurement of Coil Springs

Project at Interdisciplinary Center for Artificial Intelligence ICAI in collaboration with Spuhl GmbH 2022 - 2023

Sp체hl GmbH is the industry leader in pocket spring wire-forming machines. Accurate measurements of produced coil springs are essential for quality control. The ICAI developed an algorithm for fast and accurate 3D spring modeling with data from multiple cameras. The project started in 2020 and has been extended for the development of an industrial prototype. Details are disclosed, as a patent is pending.

Python Linux Tensorflow NumPy OpenCV
Fitted mathematical 3D model (colored dots) overlaid on the camera image. Created by the author.

Coil springs must be measured accurately during production to ensure quality standards. However, most geometries are very complex and are prone to deformation even with very little force applied. While current measuring methods can only provide limited information about the true geometry, commercially available 3D scanners are expensive and only provide incomplete output geometry.

In the course of this project, an alternative 3D scanning approach using cameras with non-orthographic optics has been developed and tested. Multiple calibrated cameras are used to simultaneously record images of a coil spring from different perspectives. Parameters of a high-dimensional mathematical model are then fitted to footage to yield a complete 3D wire path model of the spring. Furthermore, artificial intelligence accelerator hardware enables parallel stochastic optimization of the coil spring model parameters, which reduces computation time drastically and increases accuracy.

The functionality of the new measurement method has been successfully validated on demonstrator hardware. The object under test is placed on a backlit glass surface where it is captured by cameras and measured within seconds. A repeatable accuracy in the sub-millimeter range is achieved over a broad band of spring types and sizes. The measurements are repeatable and consistent over different rotations and translations on the measurement plate. The project is being pursued by Sp체hl GmbH for industrialization, where a prototype is in production.

Localization of Avalanche Transceivers

Master Thesis at OST Eastern Switzerland University of Applied Sciences 2022

Feasibility study for an extended Kalman filter based localization algorithm for 457kHz avalanche transceivers. The study has been conducted on behalf of GPV Switzerland, the manufacturer of the Barryvox avalanche transceiver. The results show that the novel algorithm is faster and easier to use than current implementations.

Python JavaScript Linux JAX NumPy ThreeJs C STM32
Monte Carlo simulation of the algorithm. Created by the author.

Backcountry skiing and snowshoe hiking are popular winter sports that involve the risk of avalanches. Avalanche transceivers are often used as a fast and reliable tool to locate buried victims. A quick and accurate localization of the victim is crucial to increase the chance of survival, which declines rapidly after a few minutes. The device carried by the victim operates as a sender, whereas the device of the rescuer is in receiving mode. Every second, a 457kHz radio pulse is emitted which is sensed by the receiver device. Current solutions guide the rescuer to follow the magnetic field lines toward the sender, which is an indirect and time-consuming path. Once the rescuer is close to the receiver, a sequence of predefined motions needs to be performed to precisely pinpoint the sender device.

This thesis suggests faster and more accurate localization techniques utilizing statistical signal processing algorithms. Our work introduces two algorithms using extended Kalman filters that differ from the type of used sensors. The physical model of the magnetic field is embodied in the filter to estimate the location and attitude of the sender relative to the receiver device. A first simplified algorithm relies on positional data, which is difficult to obtain on a low-power handheld device. The second algorithm relies only on already available sensor measurements of the Mammut Barryvox avalanche transceiver device. Both proposed algorithms have been tested extensively in a Monte Carlo simulation environment. The environment simulates the emitted magnetic field, randomized rescuer movement patterns, sensor inaccuracies, and noise levels. Furthermore, a functioning hardware demonstrator has been built to test the algorithms in the field using live data from the Barryvox device. The algorithms running on a desktop computer gather sensor data wirelessly from the hardware demonstrator. The instruction outputs are then transmitted to a graphical interface for the rescuer.

Both proposed algorithms perform extremely well in the simulation environment. The sender position estimate gets progressively more accurate during the search phase and omits the need for a pinpoint phase altogether. In the simulated environment, the hardware-adapted algorithm is able to accurately locate the victim within 0.3m horizontal distance median or 0.6m distance in 90% of all simulation runs. This accuracy is achieved in fewer than 70 simulated seconds. The hardware demonstrator is able to confirm these results, with only a slight reduction of accuracy. When following only the instructions on the graphical interface, test subjects have been able to locate the sender in 9 of 10 cases with a mean horizontal distance of 0.6m.

Detection of Invasive Neophytes using Novel Artificial Intelligence Algorithms

Master Semester Project at OST Eastern Switzerland University of Applied Sciences 2021

Invasive Neophytes are a major threat to biodiversity. This study suggests methods to detect neophytes from aerial images using machine learning techniques.

Python Tensorflow ScikitLearn
Aerial image of the Entensee in RGB and multispectral bands on the left. The classification results of the superpixel and convolutional neural network approaches are shown on the right. Created by the author.

Solidago gigantea is an invasive neophyte and a major threat to biodiversity. Our newly developed AI algorithms make it possible to detect Solidago gigantea coverage from simple RGB aerial drone imagery alone. As drone imagery becomes increasingly ubiquitous, techniques such as the proposed algorithms enable fast, cheap and accurate neophyte detection.

Our algorithms, trained with Solidago coverage labels, are able to process orthomosaic images and output a coverage map. The software is generic and hence can include additional layers, such as multispectral imagery and/or digital surface models, leading to even better results. In this work, several algorithms have been developed, including a custom deep convolutional neural network and an innovative semi-supervised clustering approach. The custom deep convolutional neural network is a fully convolutional architecture that gives a probability distribution output per pixel. As labels are often rare, the semi-supervised approach extracts features from superpixels and clusters them in an unsupervised fashion. The labels are only used to determine which unsupervised class belongs to which predefined class.

These results have been achieved on a single image from the Entensee in Kaltbrunn, Switzerland where Solidago gigantea is common. To generalize these results, aerial data and Solidago gigantea labels have been found for the whole canton of Z체rich. Unfortunately, negative labels are not available, so the algorithms could not be applied. To solve this problem, new one-class Solidago detection algorithms have been developed.

The results, although preliminary, clearly indicate the high effectiveness of the proposed solutions for identifying Solidago gigantea coverage. Since the algorithms perform well with RGB data alone, numerous potential applications using crowd-sourced data may emerge, utilizing variants of the proposed schemes.
